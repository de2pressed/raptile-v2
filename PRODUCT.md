# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Style-conscious shoppers browsing Raptile Studio on mobile and desktop, usually discovering a limited collection, comparing garment colors and sizes, then deciding whether to buy now or return later. They are reading the brand through the interface as much as they are completing a transaction, so the site has to feel editorial and controlled while staying friction-light for product selection, cart review, and support pages.

## Product Purpose
Raptile Studio is a headless Shopify storefront for a fashion label with a dark, material-led point of view. The site exists to present a tightly curated collection, let product imagery carry the emotional weight, and turn browsing into purchase without breaking the mood. Success looks like a storefront that feels premium and unmistakably branded, while core commerce actions such as color selection, size selection, add to cart, and checkout remain obvious and reliable.

## Brand Personality
Restrained, tactile, cinematic. The voice should feel deliberate and composed, with quiet confidence instead of hype. Visual drama comes from garment photography, contrast, pacing, and motion, not from loud UI chrome.

## Anti-references
Do not let the storefront drift into generic SaaS gradients, glowing dashboard cards, loud streetwear neon, playful DTC pastels, or white-label Shopify templates. Avoid decorative amber fill on secondary UI, hover gimmicks, icon-card grids, and any interface treatment that competes with the product images.

## Design Principles
1. Product imagery is the color in the room: UI chrome stays dark, warm, and restrained so garments and typography hold attention.
2. Editorial presence, transactional clarity: the collection and PDP should feel composed and fashion-led, while selection, cart, and support actions stay legible and familiar.
3. Motion should feel mechanical and premium: shutter cuts, subtle fades, and purposeful transitions over playful animation.
4. Variation creates rhythm: asymmetry, spacing contrast, and image scale shifts should prevent template repetition without harming usability.
5. Every surface should feel intentional under low visual light: nothing pure black, nothing pure white, nothing overly saturated unless it signals action.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Support reduced-motion users across all page and component transitions. Maintain strong contrast on dark surfaces, preserve keyboard focus visibility on all interactive controls, use hover color changes as enhancement rather than the sole state indicator, and keep mobile typography above cramped thresholds so browsing and purchasing remain readable on small screens.
